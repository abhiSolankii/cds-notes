### Key Borrowings from the United States of America (USA)

| Concept                            | Explanation & Indian Context                                                          |
| ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Preamble**                       | Introductory statement outlining the philosophy and values of the Constitution.       |
| **Fundamental Rights**             | Basic rights guaranteed to every citizen at birth, inspired by the US Bill of Rights. |
| **Federal Structure of Govt**      | Divides power between a central (federal) authority and constituent political units.  |
| **Judicial Review**                | Power of judiciary to nullify laws violating the Constitution’s basic structure.      |
| **President as Supreme Commander** | The President is the supreme commander of the armed forces.                           |
| **Equal Protection under Law**     | Equality of all individuals before the law.                                           |
| **Independence of Judiciary**      | Judiciary functions independently without government pressure.                        |
| **Impeachment of President**       | Process to remove the President from office.                                          |
| **Removal of Judges**              | Procedure to remove Supreme Court and High Court judges.                              |
| **Vice President post**            | Existence and role of the Vice President.                                             |

- Most of the Judiciary features are taken from USA.

### Borrowings from the United Kingdom (UK)

| Concept                              | Explanation                                                                        |
| ------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Parliamentary Form of Government** | India adopted the parliamentary system, where elected representatives make laws.   |
| **Single Citizenship**               | Citizens have only Indian citizenship, no separate state citizenship.              |
| **Cabinet System**                   | Executive authority vested in a council of ministers headed by the Prime Minister. |
| **Writ Jurisdiction (Article 32)**   | Right to approach Supreme Court directly for violation of fundamental rights.      |
| **Institution of Speaker**           | Role and powers of the Speaker in the Parliament.                                  |
| **Legislative Procedures**           | Rules and procedures for passing laws in Parliament.                               |
| **Bicameralism (Two Houses)**        | Parliament consists of two houses – Lok Sabha and Rajya Sabha.                     |

- Head of Cabinet ministers is Prime minister.

### Borrowings from Government of India Act, 1935

- Over **50% of the Indian Constitution’s provisions** are derived from this Act, including:
  - Judicial structure
  - Administrative details
  - Federal scheme
  - Emergency provisions
  - Public Service Commissions
  - Office of Governor

### Salient Features of the Indian Constitution

- **Longest Constitution in the world** with detailed provisions:
  - Initially 395 Articles, 8 Schedules, 1 Preamble
  - Now 12 Schedules, 470 articles.
- **Blend of Rigidity and Flexibility**:
  - Constitution is **partially rigid and partially flexible** (partially amendable, but basic structure cannot be altered).
  - Article 368 allows amendments but **“basic structure” remains inviolable**.
- **Independent Judiciary System**:
  - Supreme Court, High Courts, and subordinate courts form a hierarchy.
  - Judiciary is **independent and free from governmental pressure**.
  - Appointment of judges is done via the **Collegium system**.
  - Removal of judges is made deliberately difficult to prevent political influence.
- **Balance between Parliamentary Sovereignty and Judicial Supremacy**:
  - Parliament has exclusive power to make laws.
  - Judiciary has the power of **judicial review** to invalidate unconstitutional laws.
  - Constitution is supreme above institutions and individuals.
- **Parliamentary Form of Government**:
  - Majority party forms government.
  - Prime Minister and Council of Ministers are responsible to Lok Sabha.
  - Lok Sabha is the lower house with powers to dissolve and call elections every 5 years.
  - Collective responsibility of ministers towards the legislature.
- **Federal System with Unitary Features**:
  - Strong Centre with defined powers for states.
  - Division of powers in Union, State, and Concurrent Lists (7th Schedule).
  - Emergency provisions strengthen Centre’s role.
  - Single Constitution and single citizenship.
  - Appointment of Governors by Centre.
  - No equality in state representation in Lok Sabha.
  - All India Services controlled by Centre.
- **Democratic System**:
  - Free and fair elections monitored by Election Commission.
  - Universal adult franchise (18+ years) without communal or religious bias.
- **Union of States**:
  - India is a union of states, and no state can secede or claim independent nationhood.
  - Right to secession is denied explicitly.
- **Fundamental Rights**:
  - Justiciable rights ensuring individual liberty and equality.
  - Enforceable by courts under Articles 32 and 226.
- **Directive Principles of State Policy**:
  - Non-justiciable guidelines for social and economic democracy.
  - Aim to establish a welfare state.
- **Fundamental Duties**:
  - Moral obligations of citizens towards nation and society.
  - Added by the 42nd Constitutional Amendment.
- **Secular State**:
  - No state religion; equal respect to all religions.
  - Freedom of conscience and worship guaranteed.
- **Universal Adult Franchise**:
  - Right to vote granted to all citizens above 18 years.
  - Based on equality and political justice.
- **Emergency Provisions**:
  - Three types: National, State, and Financial Emergency.
  - Temporary concentration of power in Centre during crisis.
- **Single Citizenship**:
  - All citizens enjoy uniform political and civil rights.
- **Rule of Law**:
  - Supremacy of law over arbitrary authority.
  - Equality before law ensured.
