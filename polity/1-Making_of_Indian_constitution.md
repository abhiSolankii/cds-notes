### Key Highlights and Insights

- **Indian Constitution: History and Uniqueness**:

  - Indiaâ€™s Constitution is considered one of the most **unique** and comprehensive documents globally, designed to unify a diverse country with multiple religions, languages, and castes.
  - Indian Prime minister is nominated by elected Member of parliaments.
  - The Constitution was developed during British colonial rule, reflecting the Indian aspiration for **Swaraj (self-rule)**.
  - Key figures: **Dr. B.R. Ambedkar** (Father of the Indian Constitution), **M.N. Roy** (who first proposed the Constituent Assembly), **Jawaharlal Nehru**, **Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel**, and others played crucial roles.

- **Historical Milestones**:
  - Early demands for a Constitution were raised in the early 1900s, intensified post-1920s.
  - **Swaraj Bill** was presented by Bal Gangadhar Tilak in **1895**.
  - In **1922**. Gandhi ji said Swaraj will be declaration of India's full self expression and rule.
  - In **1934**. **M.N Roy** first proposed idea of Constituent assembly.
- **Three round table conferences(1930-1932)**:

  - First conference **(1930-1931)**
  - Second conference **(Sep 1931 - Dec 1931)**
  - Third Conference **(Nov 1932 - Dec 1932)**
  - **Dr. B.R. Ambedkar and Tej Bhupendra Sapru** Attended all three conferences.

- **Constituent Assembly**:

  - When all the conferences failed. In **1935** INC demanded constituent assembly officially.
  - In **1938**. Jawaharlal Nehru again demanded constituent assembly but based on Universal adult franchise. Without external Interference. Britishers rejected becase they wanted to interfere.
  - **1934-1945. WWII happened.** And because India was colony they forced Indians to go in war.

- **August offer (1940)**
  - When Viceroy Linlithgow was Viceroy and Winston churchill was PM there.
  - British govt proposed a dominion status. (But Indians wanted complete freedom).
  - Defence, Finance, Home affairs and republic services will be under British and complete independence was refused at all.
  - At wardha, **INC rejected the August offer**. Nehru said "Dominion status is dead as a door nail".
  - Gandhi ji said "Declaration has widened the gap between the nationalists and the British rulers."
- **1942 Crispps Mission**:

  - When Japan started dominating in WWII.
  - Headed by **Stafford Cripps**.
  - Accepted elected constituent assembly. Also Dominion status, an equal partnership of British commonwealth of Nation. But defence will be under british. Also no date was provided and all this will be done after WWII they promised.
  - **Right to secede** will be there. Any state can separate by voting if not happy with constition.
  - This offer was rejected. Even Muslim league rejected it with INC. And Gandhi said "A post dated cheque".

- **Making of Constituend assembly and meetings**:

  - **First meeting (9 December 1946)**:

    - The Constituent Assembly held its first meeting on **9 December 1946** in **New Delhi** and only 211 members attended.
    - The first temporary president was **Dr. Sachchidananda Sinha**; later, **Dr. Rajendra Prasad** was elected permanent president in second meeting.

  - **Second meeting(11 December 1946)**:

    - **Dr. Rajendra prasad** was elected as President.
    - Later becamse first President of India.
    - There were two elected Vice presidents. **H.c Mookerjee and V.T Krishnamachari**.

  - **Third meeting (13 December 1946)**:
    - The Assembly presented an **Objective Resolution** (Jawaharlal Nehru), laying down the guiding principles of the Constitution and vision (preamble) of Constitution was decided.
    - The objective resolution was passed on **22 Jan 1947**.
    - Drafting involved **eight major committees** and **thirteen minor committees**, with the **Drafting Committee** headed by Dr. B.R. Ambedkar.
    - The Constitution was adopted on **26 November 1949** and came into effect on **26 January 1950**.

- **Mountbatten Plan (1947)**:

  - After partition Out of 389 only 299 members were remaining.
  - The Mountbatten Plan (June 3 Plan) of 1947 was Lord Mountbatten's blueprint for British India's swift transfer of power, leading to its partition into India and Pakistan on August 15, 1947, with provisions for provinces like Punjab and Bengal to divide by legislative assembly vote, referendums in NWFP and Sylhet, and a Boundary Commission (headed by Radcliffe) to draw borders, granting princely states freedom to choose their accession.

- **Important Schedules Explained**:

  - **1st Schedule**: Names and territories of States and Union Territories.

    - Deals with territorial changes of states and UTs.
    - **7th Constitutional Amendment Act, 1956**:
      - Formation of **Andhra Pradesh** (first linguistic state).

  - **2nd Schedule**: Salaries and allowances of key constitutional posts.

    - President
    - Governors
    - Judges of Supreme Court and High Courts
    - Speaker and Deputy Speaker of Lok Sabha
    - Chairman and Deputy Chairman of Rajya Sabha
    - Comptroller and Auditor General (CAG)

  - **3rd Schedule**: Forms of oaths and affirmations.

    - Union Ministers
    - Members of Parliament (MPs)
    - Candidates contesting elections
    - Judges of Supreme Court and High Courts
    - State Ministers and Members of State Legislature

  - **4th Schedule**: Allocation of seats in the Rajya Sabha.

    - Seats allotted to States and Union Territories.

  - **5th Schedule**: Administration and control of Scheduled Areas and Scheduled Tribes.

    - Applies to most tribal areas except those under the 6th Schedule.
    - **Only the President** can declare a Scheduled Area.

  - **6th Schedule**: Special provisions for tribal areas.

    - Applies to **Assam, Meghalaya, Tripura, Mizoram**.
    - Provides **Autonomous District Councils (ADCs)**.
    - Related Articles:
      - **Article 244(2)**
      - **Article 275(1)** (grants for tribal welfare)

  - **7th Schedule**: Division of powers between Union and States.

    - **Union List**:
      - Subjects like Defence, Atomic Energy, Foreign Affairs, Banking, Railways, Currency.
      - Originally: 97 subjects
      - Currently: **100 subjects**
    - **State List**:
      - Subjects like Police, Public Health, Agriculture, Land, Liquor.
      - Originally: 66 subjects
      - Currently: **61 subjects**
    - **Concurrent List**:
      - Subjects like Education, Forests, Trade Unions, Marriage, Succession.
      - Originally: 47 subjects
      - Currently: **52 subjects**
    - **42nd Amendment Act, 1976** shifted the following from State List to Concurrent List:
      - Education
      - Forests
      - Protection of wild animals and birds
      - Weights and measures
      - Administration of justice

  - **Residual Powers**:

    - Subjects not mentioned in Union, State, or Concurrent List.
    - **Article 248**:
      - Residual powers belong to the **Union Parliament**.

  - **8th Schedule**: Official languages of India.

    - Contains **22 languages**.
    - Originally: 14 languages.
    - Added later:
      - **Sindhi** â€“ 21st Amendment Act, 1967
      - **Konkani, Manipuri, Nepali** â€“ 71st Amendment Act, 1992
      - **Bodo, Dogri, Maithili, Santhali** â€“ 92nd Amendment Act, 2003

  - **Classical Languages of India**:

    - Tamil (2004)
    - Sanskrit (2005)
    - Kannada (2008)
    - Telugu (2008)
    - Malayalam (2013)
    - Odia (2014)

  - **9th Schedule**: Protection of certain laws from judicial review.

    - Mainly land reform laws.
    - Abolition of Zamindari System.
    - Added by **1st Constitutional Amendment Act, 1951**.

  - **10th Schedule**: Anti-Defection Law.

    - Disqualification of MPs and MLAs on grounds of defection.
    - Added by **52nd Constitutional Amendment Act, 1985**.
    - Strengthened by **91st Amendment Act, 2003**.

  - **11th Schedule**: Powers and responsibilities of Panchayats.

    - Contains **29 subjects**.
    - Added by **73rd Constitutional Amendment Act, 1992**.

  - **12th Schedule**: Powers and responsibilities of Municipalities.
    - It has 18 matters.
    - Added by **74th Constitutional Amendment Act, 1992**.
